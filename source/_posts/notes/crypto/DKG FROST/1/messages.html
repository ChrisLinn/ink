Coming late to the party: we must be careful of the wording "round" here. Gennaro in this paper says Pedersen DKG has "one round of communication in the absence of faults" - that's correct but there are still _two_ rounds when looking at the time: There's always the share distribution phase and the response phase. Then it can either stops or continue to justification phase if there is some complaints. But all nodes need to wait until the end of the response phase (so this is the second "round" in time) in any cases.
