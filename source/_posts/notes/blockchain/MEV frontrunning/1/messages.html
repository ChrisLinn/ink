In reply to this message
A recent paper on Delay Encryption https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/638.pdf may solve this problem. In the delay encryption scheme, deriving the secret key (used for decryption) from the public key is time-locked, rather than decrypting the ciphertext. To apply delay encryption in this inclusion-decryption scenario, the contract can collect all encrypted txs in a fixed time period, then everyone can start revealing the secret key (which is time-locked). As secret key will eventually be known by everyone and decryption is fast, selectively decrypting txs won't lead to attacks.

Does anyone know of an implementation (or even a simple algorithm description) to efficiently compute a batch multiexponentation. By batch multiexponentation I mean that you have to compute n > 1 multiexponentiations, each of size k, and each multiexponentiation shares the same base (e.g. the same Bulletproof basis, or the same powers of Tau).

I know that, at least asymptotically, Pippenger's algorithm computes an (n, k) batch multiexponentiation at roughly the same cost of a single multiexponentiation of size n*k. This claim stems from the first page of the Bernstein paper (see screenshot) on Pippenger's algorithm https://cr.yp.to/papers/pippenger.ps.
